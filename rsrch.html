Purpose:

I worked with a UCSD professor to increase the accessibility for his epidemic simulation. End-to-end simulations require sometimes more than 50 difficult-to-find parameters, so they can be intimidating to approach.

Ideas:

Configuration files for specific diseases and in-depth READMEs could help users reason through examples that they can apply to their own research. Then, they can run their own simulations by changing around parameters!

Execution:

I reviewed literature to pull parameters for HIV and COVID. For example, I reviewed a paper about HIV progression for patients on ART and converted what I learned there into a Poisson process as a part of an already-created COVID network framework. For each parameter, I explained my reasoning. I also wrote a pre-print talking the improved user-friendliness of the simulation, along with my friend who created a webapp to visualize the simulated data. Check out the pre-print to the left!
